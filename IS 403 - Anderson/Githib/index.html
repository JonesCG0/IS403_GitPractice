Engine Overview

The 6.0 Powerstroke diesel engine, produced from 2003 to 2007, powered Ford Super Duty trucks and Excursions. Known for its responsive power delivery and emission innovations, it also gained a reputation for a few critical components that require attention. This guide highlights the key facts enthusiasts and owners need to know.

Key Specifications

  • Displacement: 365 cubic inches (6.0L)
  • Configuration: 90-degree V8 turbo diesel
  • Horsepower: 325 hp @ 3,300 RPM
  • Torque: 560 lb-ft @ 2,000 RPM
  • Fuel System: HEUI (Hydraulically Actuated Electronically Controlled Unit Injector)
  • Turbocharger: Garrett GT3782VA variable geometry turbo

Common Trouble Spots

Head Gaskets & Studs

Factory torque-to-yield head bolts can stretch under high boost. Upgrading to ARP head studs is a popular reliability modification.

EGR System

EGR coolers may crack and leak coolant. Regular cleaning and ensuring the oil cooler is healthy helps prevent failure.

Oil Cooler

Clogged passages restrict flow to the high-pressure oil pump. Coolant filtration and proper maintenance extend service life.

Essential Maintenance Intervals

Service Item Interval Notes
Engine Oil & Filter 5,000 miles Use CJ-4 rated 15W-40 diesel oil and Motorcraft filters.
Fuel Filters 10,000 miles Replace both primary and secondary filters; prime system before start.
Coolant Flush Every 45,000 miles Switch to CAT EC-1 rated coolant and add a filtration kit.
FICM Voltage Check Every oil change Ensure voltage stays at or above 48V to protect injectors.
